# I Like JPGs — Milestone 0 Validation Plan

## Purpose

Milestone 0 is a validation sprint.

The goal is to prove that collectors, artists, curators, and community people are interested in a taste-based read of an NFT wallet.

This is not yet about building the full product. It is about learning whether the core promise creates enough curiosity for someone to enter or share a wallet.

## Core Question

Would someone want to see what their NFT wallet says about their taste?

## Product Promise

I Like JPGs gives people a cultural read of an NFT wallet.

Not portfolio value.  
Not rarity scores.  
Not floor prices.  
Not trading advice.

A read on taste, culture, collecting behavior, and visible patterns.

## Milestone 0 Includes

1. A lightweight landing / concept page
2. A wallet input or interest CTA
3. Manual taste report examples
4. A taste taxonomy
5. An interview guide
6. A lightweight technical proof that a wallet can produce a live taste read

## Milestone 0 Does Not Include

- Wallet comparison
- Collector matching
- Nearby collectors
- Social graph
- Account creation
- Saved profiles
- Messaging
- Market value
- Floor prices
- Offers
- Rarity
- Trading language

## Validation Audience

Primary:

- NFT collectors
- Artists
- Curators
- Community holders
- Project/community leads

Secondary:

- People interested in digital identity
- People interested in cultural graphs
- People who may not trade NFTs often but understand wallets as public taste records

## What We Are Testing

### 1. Concept Clarity

Do people understand what I Like JPGs does?

Signal:

- They can explain it back simply.
- They understand it is about taste, not value.

### 2. Desire

Are people curious enough to enter or share a wallet?

Signal:

- They say yes to “Would you want to see yours?”
- They send a wallet without needing much convincing.

### 3. Accuracy

Does the read feel believable?

Signal:

- They recognize themselves in the report.
- They agree with the top collections, categories, and proof NFTs.
- They correct details rather than dismiss the premise.

### 4. Social Pull

Does the read make them want to compare, share, or discover others?

Signal:

- They ask how they compare to another collector.
- They want to see friends, artists, or communities.
- They imagine using it at events, clubs, or collector gatherings.

### 5. Trust

Does the product feel respectful?

Signal:

- It feels interpretive, not creepy.
- It does not overclaim identity.
- It does not reduce the wallet to value or status.

## Success Criteria

Milestone 0 is successful if:

- People understand the concept quickly.
- At least several people are willing to enter or share a wallet.
- Manual taste reports feel directionally accurate.
- The live read can produce a credible first result.
- The product creates interest in Milestone 1: comparing two wallets.

## Strong Success Signal

Someone says some version of:

> “I want to see mine.”

Or:

> “I want to compare this with someone else.”

## Interview Goal

The interview should learn:

- What people already look for in wallets
- Whether they believe wallets carry taste signals
- What would make a read feel accurate
- What would make it feel wrong
- Whether they would share or compare the result
- Whether this could help them discover people, artists, collections, clubs, or communities

## Prototype Experience

The Milestone 0 prototype should show:

1. What I Like JPGs is
2. Why wallet taste matters
3. What a taste read includes
4. A few manual taste report examples
5. A way to enter or share a wallet
6. A clear next step toward live wallet reading

## Positioning Notes

The product should feel:

- Cultural
- Visual
- Social
- Interpretive
- Playful
- Lightweight
- Human

It should not feel:

- Financial
- Forensic
- Overbuilt
- Marketplace-like
- Ranking-based
- Status-obsessed

## Guiding Line

Collections are the proof.  
Categories are the meaning.  
Formats are the behavior.  
Traits and artists are the texture.
