I Like JPGs — Milestone 0 Validation Plan
Purpose

Milestone 0 is a validation sprint.

The goal is to prove that people are interested in using NFT collections as taste signals to discover aligned collectors, artists, communities, and cultural overlap.

This is not yet the full product. It is a focused test of whether people understand and want a lightweight collector-discovery experience built around what they like.

Core Question

Would someone choose a few NFT collections they like to see which wallets and collectors are culturally nearby?

Product Promise

I Like JPGs helps people find collectors through shared taste.

Not portfolio value.
Not rarity scores.
Not floor prices.
Not trading advice.
Not status ranking.

A playful cultural map based on collections, artists, aesthetics, communities, and visible wallet patterns.

Core Interaction

The first interaction is not:

“Connect your wallet.”

The first interaction is:

“Pick a few collections you like.”

From there, I Like JPGs shows:

“Here are collectors near this taste.”

This keeps the experience lower-friction, more exploratory, and more social.

What We Are Testing
1. Concept Clarity

Do people understand the idea quickly?

Signal:

They can explain it back as “pick collections, find similar collectors.”
They understand it is about taste and cultural overlap, not value.
They understand collections are being used as signals.
2. Interaction Desire

Are people curious enough to pick collections?

Signal:

They start typing collection names.
They select multiple collections without needing explanation.
They ask what happens after selecting them.
They want to try combinations.
3. Collection as Identity Signal

Do people believe collection choices say something meaningful?

Signal:

They say things like “that does feel like my taste.”
They care which collections are included.
They debate whether a collection belongs in their taste set.
They ask to add artists, editions, communities, or ecosystems.
4. Discovery Pull

Does the selected taste set make people want to see other wallets?

Signal:

They ask “who else collects this?”
They want to see top matching wallets.
They want to find artists, friends, collectors, or community people.
They imagine using it at events, clubs, drops, or gatherings.
5. Trust

Does the experience feel respectful and accurate enough?

Signal:

The selected collections feel like real proof.
The product does not overclaim identity.
It does not feel creepy, financialized, or judgmental.
People understand they are choosing signals intentionally.
Success Criteria

Milestone 0 is successful if:

People understand the product in under 30 seconds.
People willingly select collections.
The collection picker feels trustworthy.
Selected collections feel like meaningful taste signals.
People want to see matching wallets after choosing collections.
At least several people say they would use this to find collectors, artists, or communities.
The product creates interest in Milestone 1: real wallet matching from selected collection holders.
Strong Success Signals

Someone says some version of:

“I want to see who else likes these.”

Or:

“I want to find wallets like this.”

Or:

“I’d use this at an event.”

Or:

“Can I compare this with my wallet?”

Prototype Flow
Step 1: Landing

Explain the concept simply:

Pick a few collections you like.
Find collectors near your taste.

Support copy:

NFTs are public signals of what people notice, value, and return to. I Like JPGs uses those signals to help collectors find cultural overlap.

Step 2: Collection Picker

User types collection names.

Important rule:

Typing is search. Selection is commitment.

The app should only add collections that come from real collection result objects.

No raw slug guessing.
No arbitrary text.
No fake collections.

Step 3: Selected Taste Set

The selected collections become a visible taste query.

Each card should show:

collection image
name
slug
contract if useful
remove option
Step 4: Results CTA

Once collections are selected:

Find collectors with this taste

Step 5: Results Shell

The results page should frame the promise even before full holder discovery exists:

Collectors near this taste

Show selected collections and an intentional placeholder:

Wallet matching is coming next. This taste set will be used to find collectors who hold overlapping collections.

Or, if using manual/demo data:

These are example wallets that overlap with this taste set.

Validation Questions
Concept
What do you think this does?
Does “pick collections, find collectors near your taste” make sense?
Does this feel different from a marketplace or analytics tool?
Collection Selection
Which collections would you pick first?
Would you pick collections you own, collections you admire, or both?
Does choosing collections feel easier than entering a wallet?
What would make a collection feel like a strong taste signal?
Discovery
After choosing collections, what would you expect to see?
Would you want to see matching wallets?
Would you want to see artists, collectors, communities, or clubs?
Would you want to know why a wallet matched?
Trust
What would make this feel accurate?
What would make it feel wrong?
Does this feel too personal, or does it feel like public cultural signal?
Should users choose what signals they share?
Future Pull
Would you connect your wallet after trying this?
Would you compare your wallet with a friend’s?
Would this be useful at NFT events or community gatherings?
Would you share a result if it felt right?
What We Should Learn

By the end of Milestone 0, we should know:

Whether collection-picking is a compelling first action
Whether people understand collections as taste coordinates
Whether people want wallet discovery from selected collections
Whether the product should lead with search, wallet input, or both
Which collection categories create the strongest reactions
Whether the phrase “collectors near this taste” lands
Whether people want matching, comparison, or personal wallet reading next
